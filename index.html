Lifestyle · Private Memberships

Country Club Directory

The private clubs of Las Vegas & Henderson — joining fees, monthly dues, guest rates, and the social life behind the gates. Tap any club to expand.

The Summit Club

Summerlin · Tom Fazio · 2015 · Homes $8M–$40M+

Course — 7,500-yard Tom Fazio championship layout carved into the Spring Mountains. Dramatic elevation, red-rock backdrop, and conditioning that's arguably the best in the state. Five-star experience from the first tee.

Amenities — Stunning clubhouse with multiple dining outlets, full spa, tennis, pickleball, resort pool, fitness center, wellness. Kids' programs, event spaces, and concierge-level service throughout.

Social / Access — The most exclusive club in Las Vegas — arguably among the top 5 most exclusive in the country. Guard-gated within Summerlin's guarded gates. Celebrity, pro-athlete, and executive membership. No photos inside. No tours. Introductions happen behind closed doors.

By Invitation · Call Brent for Summit Access

Southern Highlands Golf Club

SW Vegas · Robert Trent Jones Sr. & Jr. · 2000 · Homes $2M–$15M+

Course — 7,381-yard RTJ design with parkland fairways, mature landscaping, and one of the most pristine conditioning standards in the Southwest. Hosts the Southern Highlands Collegiate each spring.

Amenities — Expansive clubhouse with fine dining, member lounges, tennis, pool, fitness. Limited event calendar — discretion is the amenity here.

Social / Access — Arguably the most exclusive golf club in Vegas. Strong celebrity and executive membership. Invitation-based — interested buyers should budget for a long introduction process.

View Homes in Southern Highlands

DragonRidge Country Club (MacDonald Highlands)

Henderson · Jay Morrish & David Druzisky · 2000 · Homes $2M–$20M+

Course — 7,200-yard championship layout carved into Black Mountain. Dramatic elevation changes, panoramic Strip views, and a signature par-3 over a canyon. Firm, fast, and played by anyone who lives in MacDonald Highlands.

Amenities — Full clubhouse with fine dining and casual grill, 8 tennis/pickleball courts, fitness center, spa, resort pool, private event spaces.

Social / Access — Active calendar of member tournaments, wine dinners, holiday events, and couples golf. Reasonable dress code enforcement. One of the more social ultra-luxury clubs in Henderson.

View Homes in MacDonald Highlands

TPC Summerlin

Summerlin · Bobby Weed + Fuzzy Zoeller · 1991 · Homes $1M–$4M

Course — 7,255-yard PGA Tour host. Immaculate conditioning year-round, strategic bunkering, and the kind of demanding tee-to-green routing that rewards shot-making. Hosts the Shriners Children's Open every fall.

Amenities — Classic tour-style clubhouse with member dining and pro shop. Less resort-y, more golf-focused. No tennis, no spa — the draw is the course.

Social / Access — The serious-golfer's club. Strong member golf calendar, tournaments, and guest days. Tour-pro access during the Shriners Open week is a real member perk.

View Homes Near TPC Summerlin

Red Rock Country Club

Summerlin · Arnold Palmer Design · 2003 · Homes $1M–$5M+

Course — Two 18-hole Palmer Design courses — Mountain (bold, desert-rugged, with Red Rock backdrop) and Arroyo (tree-lined, strategic, tighter). 36 holes under one club is rare in Vegas.

Amenities — Stunning clubhouse with panoramic Red Rock views, multiple dining outlets, tennis, pickleball, swim/fitness complex, kids' programs.

Social / Access — Strongest family vibe of any Summerlin CC. Kids' junior golf, swim team, and tennis programs. Full social calendar — wine events, holiday parties, member golf.

View Homes in Red Rock CC

Anthem Country Club

Henderson · Hale Irwin · 1999 · Homes $800K–$3M

Course — One of Hale Irwin's early residential designs — 7,267-yard layout with rolling fairways, strategic bunkering, and one of the better-conditioned greens complexes in Henderson. Playable for all skill levels.

Amenities — Full amenity club — tennis (multiple courts), pickleball, resort pool, fitness center, spa, multiple dining outlets, event space. Social-only membership tier available.

Social / Access — Arguably the most active social calendar in Henderson — weekly events, tournaments, book clubs, cards, wine dinners. Heavy active-retiree presence, but also strong with young families and snowbirds.

View Homes in Anthem Country Club

Spanish Trail Country Club

SW Vegas · Robert Trent Jones Jr. · 1985 · Homes $700K–$4M

Course — 27 holes (three 9-hole loops: Lakes, Canyon, Sunrise) by RTJ Jr. Vegas's first RTJ Jr. design — 40+ years of mature trees, established parkland conditioning, and classic routing.

Amenities — Clubhouse with fine dining and grill room, tennis, swim, fitness. Less spa/resort focused, more traditional country club.

Social / Access — The established-Vegas play. Strong multi-generational membership — families that have been members 20+ years. Classic country club traditions intact. Dress code enforced.

View Homes in Spanish Trail

SouthShore Country Club

Henderson · Lake Las Vegas · Jack Nicklaus · 1996 · Homes $1M–$10M+

Course — Jack Nicklaus Signature design on the south shore of Lake Las Vegas. 7,000+ yards of lakeside fairways, desert-transition holes, and dramatic Mediterranean-style clubhouse views. The private counterpart to the public-facing Reflection Bay next door.

Amenities — Private clubhouse with fine dining, event space, lakefront terrace. Access to the broader Lake Las Vegas resort amenities (marina, spa, hotels) via reciprocal agreements.

Social / Access — Members-only, no public play. Lake Las Vegas homeowners have preferred access. The quieter, more private side of Lake LV golf — distinctly separate from Reflection Bay's resort scene.

View Homes at Lake Las Vegas

Las Vegas Country Club

Central LV · Edmund Ault · 1967 · Homes $700K–$3M+

Course — Vegas's original private country club — opened 1967, making it the oldest private course in the valley. Mature trees, established conditioning, and classic parkland routing. Hosted PGA Tour's Sahara/Las Vegas Invitational for years in the '70s and '80s.

Amenities — Full clubhouse with fine dining and grill room, tennis courts, pool, fitness, event space. Traditional country club feel with deep history.

Social / Access — The establishment play. Multigenerational membership, old-Vegas traditions, and a location that's minutes to the Strip. Less flashy than the newer clubs — which is exactly the point.

View Homes in Las Vegas Country Club

Bear's Best Las Vegas (The Ridges)

Summerlin · Jack Nicklaus · 2001 · Homes $1.5M–$15M+

Course — 18 holes replicating Nicklaus's favorite holes from his global portfolio — play Muirfield Village, Castle Pines, Valhalla, and more in a single round. 7,200 yards carved through desert with Red Rock as the backdrop.

Amenities — Upscale clubhouse with dining, pro shop, and event space. No tennis or pool — this is a golf-first facility.

Social / Access — Not a membership club in the traditional sense — Ridges homeowners can often arrange preferred access, otherwise it's daily-fee with premium pricing. No initiation fees required to play.

View Homes in The Ridges

Canyon Gate Country Club

Summerlin · Ted Robinson · 1989 · Homes $700K–$2.5M

Course — Ted Robinson design with water features on 10 holes. 6,700 yards — more playable than demanding, ideal for mixed-skill groups. One of the west valley's original gated golf courses.

Amenities — Clubhouse with casual dining, tennis courts, pool, fitness. More modest than Red Rock or TPC but price reflects that.

Social / Access — Friendly, lower-key private club vibe. Strong starter-club or downsize destination. Lower initiation makes gated golf life actually attainable.

View Homes in Canyon Gate
